Clannit is a Nigerian app focused on estate and community security, a delicate and essential mission. While the functionality of the app serves its purpose, the existing design feels outdated and lacks the emotional connection required for a product that handles something as critical as safety and trust. My goal with this redesign was to create a cleaner and more modern interface that balances usability and emotional resonance, while amplifying the sense of security Clannit is meant to provide.

The first screen I redesigned was the onboarding. The current onboarding flow lacks personality, which is an important aspect for an app that deals with protecting families and communities. I opted for a simpler, cleaner design that still drives an emotional connection. The screen features an image of two children together, a visual that instantly evokes care and protection, paired with the caption ‚ÄúSafety and love, all in one place.‚Äù To ensure usability, I included a skip button, a progress indicator, and a next button to guide the user seamlessly through the flow. The overall tone is simple and elegant, yet it immediately communicates the app‚Äôs purpose and values.

For the login screen, the current version displays the Clannit icon, a ‚ÄúWelcome back‚Äù text, unlabeled text fields, a contact support button, and the app version number. In my redesign, I replaced the ‚ÄúWelcome back‚Äù text with a clear ‚ÄúLog In‚Äù label so that users instantly understand the page‚Äôs function. I also introduced a darker shade of Clannit‚Äôs green to create better contrast against the clean white background, giving the screen a sharper and more polished look. Labels were added to the input fields for clarity, and I changed the ‚ÄúForgot password‚Äù link to green, aligning it to the left instead of the center so it remains visible without distracting from the primary login action. The result is a minimal and functional login screen that prioritizes clarity and ease of use.

The feed screen also required significant refinement. The existing design uses unfamiliar icons, a floating add-post button, a hamburger menu, and a pill-style selector to navigate between pages. I replaced the navbar icons with more universally recognizable ones, changing the Guests icon to multiple people, the Feed icon to a gallery, and the Invoice icon to a document instead of a credit card. The add-post button was moved from a floating position to the navbar for a cleaner layout. I removed the hamburger menu and the contact support button, moving their contents to a new ‚ÄúMore‚Äù section in the navbar, which now contains five items. Instead of the pill-style navigation, I used tabs, a more standard and familiar UX pattern. I also removed the search bar for a less cluttered interface and designed the feed to feel similar to Instagram since the features are quite close. This makes the screen intuitive and instantly familiar to most users.

The guest page needed better contrast and usability. In the original design, the Invite Guest and Track Guest tabs used a green stroke on a green background, which made them blend in and difficult to distinguish. I redesigned the tabs with a dark green stroke on a white background to create clear separation and hierarchy. Throughout the redesign, my focus was on creating an interface that feels modern, emotionally engaging, and user-first. The use of a darker shade of green across the screens helps establish trust and gives the app a more polished and professional identity.
